Gray

I tend to use gray a lot when designing web pages. Why? Because if there’s one thing I’m absolutely horrible at, it’s devising a pleasant color scheme.

That’s where gray comes in. Gray goes well with everything. It doesn’t clash with any colors, because it’s technically not a color; it’s somewhere between black and white. And it’s infinitely adaptable. When I’m at a loss for colors, I just fill in an appropriate shade of gray, always intending to replace it with a suitable color in the final layout, and always leaving it as is when I realize that I have no idea what that color should be. Entire sites end up in monochrome.

Lately, it’s been getting out of hand. I use gray so much now that I have a set of hexadecimal values preprogrammed into my brain that correspond to common web page elements. Body text? #333. Headers? #666. Light text? #999. Borders? #CCC. Light gray fill? #EEE. So not only do I use shades of gray, I use the same shades of gray over and over and over again. And I’m sick of it.

You see, when used in moderation, gray can can add to the aesthetic appeal of a site without shifting its overall color balance. When overused, however, gray is at best dull, and at worst emotionally draining.

My midyear’s resolution (if there’s such a thing) is to use less gray. I will seek professional help if necessary.
