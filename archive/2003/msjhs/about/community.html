Student and Community Profile

Mission San Jose High School is located in a residential community in the southeastern area of San Francisco Bay. It is one of five comprehensive high schools in the Fremont Unified School District, and serves 2035 students in grades nine through twelve. During the past six years, enrollment at Mission has grown from 1950 to 2035, with a concomitant increase in the ethnic diversity of the student body that is now 61% minority.

The Mission San Jose community has high expectations. Mission and its five feeder schools are widely perceived as excellent and enjoy strong community support. Five of our schools have received state or national recognition awards. Mission, itself, is a California Distinguished School and a National Blue Ribbon School in both 1987 and 1996. Mission received the 2000 Siemens High School Award for Advanced Placement. Two of Mission's staff won national awards in 2000. We had a Disney's American Teacher Award winner and a Mathematical Association of America's Edyth May Sliffle Award winner for Distinguished High School Mathematics Teaching. A community of learners involved in collaborative improvement, the school is characterized by organizational vitality, high student achievement, and the ability to function in a manner clearly contributing to the attainment of school-wide curricular and co-curricular goals.

The focus of this school is the student, and student achievement is our primary goal. Virtually every senior graduates. The attendance rate is above 96 percent. The dropout rate is below 0.5 percent. SAT scores are in the top 5 percent of the nation. Sixty-five percent of students are on the Honor Roll, and 95 percent of students go on to post-secondary schools. Each year, Mission leads Alameda County in the number of National Merit Scholar Finalists (29) and Commended Students (58). Mission's recent API scores on the California SAT-9 tests were 888 and 882, well above the level for high performing schools. Mission had the highest ranking of 10 in both state and similar school ranking.

Mission's success is based upon the precept that all students can and will learn. The tone of this school expresses respect for the learner and the process of learning. Projects that engender student participation are prevalent throughout the school day. Mission students demonstrate their mastery and in-depth learning with exhibits, projects, portfolios and performances.

In addition to a rigorous academic program for all students, we provide a number of outstanding special programs. Our award winning Special Education-Regular Education Collaborative Program has been selected as an exemplar by the State of California. Our award-winning three-tiered woodshop program is particularly exceptional, with students and teacher garnering the top county and state awards for the past eighteen consecutive years. Our students work at hundreds of different locations throughout the community under the auspices of our Work Experience and Regional Occupation Programs. We are copartners with parents and the community in the innovative Ohlone Tech Prep program, which creates a seamless articulated sequence of career pathways, grade ten to graduate school. We offer college courses on site through Ohlone College to expand the curricular choices for our students.

A varied and extensive co-curricular program involves the entire student body in a full spectrum of athletics as well as over 40 clubs and student organizations. Students take an active role in the leadership of the school, contributing to a program enriches the school and the community with public performances in drama, music, photography, drawing, and painting. Award winning student publications, a school newspaper, and literary and multicultural magazines, reflect the excellence of the school. A partnership with the Fremont Chinese School has translated to six levels of Mandarin in the foreign language curriculum. A consortium of the local ROP, community college and Cal State campus is providing Career Paths for our students in computer studies, merchandise/marketing, accounting, and business office administration, with engineering and technology, and computer aided manufacturing (CAD /CAM).

No profile of Mission High could be complete without discussion of the extent to which parents are involved in and dedicated to their children's education. During each school year, 550 Mission parents volunteer over 3,000 hours of direct support here at school. In the past one to two years, Mission parents worked in the library, cleaned and upgraded the playing fields, upgraded the campus through the purchase of picnic tables and the construction of seating areas, and raised over $200,000 in support of curricular and co-curricular programs. They actively worked to increase the involvement of minority parents in the PFA and of families joining the PFA in general. Ninety percent attended BTSN and over 60% came to Advisement Conferences. Parents provide the senior class with a full scale GRAD Nite party. They designed, implemented and paid for counselors to present "College Nights" for students and parents.

Parents recognize the staff with special events throughout the year, proctor competency, advanced placement, and Golden State Exams, help with Hearing Tests, student orientation, and pre-registration, write and publish the school newsletter on alternate months, and assemble and mail all the school newsletters. Mission San Jose High School is a Digital High School. We are in the first year of implementation and have formed a concrete vision of where we want to be in terms of technology-based instruction. Mission parents are active in the school's Technology Committee and are committed to implementing it. Many of them contributed their expertise to our 16 Net Day activities and have worked with staff and students to network the entire school.

Mission San Jose High School and the community that it serves are dedicated to building an ever-improving community of learning. During the past five years, high-test scores and other objective indicators of student success notwithstanding, we have initiated changes to align our school with the California State Standards and to provide programs and structures to better serve our students. To that end we joined the California League of High Schools. These efforts to continually improve the school have lead to interdisciplinary units, Tech Prep Career Pathways, and local partnerships for our students. Mission San Jose is school intent on providing the best for its students.
